The Basics

Hyper Text Markup Language, or HTML is the standard markup (system for processing, definition and presentation of text) language for web pages.
This "order of presentation" is done using different "tags", which makes the elements of the text you want to appear on the page look or behave differently. This basic guide shows the common ones used in most web pages.

HTML tags are written like this: '< whatever > < / whatever >'. Note the use of < > (or opening tag), and (closing tag), and they are paired. Some HTML tags are also self closing, but more often than not, they have an opening and a closing tag.

Headings

There are six (6) levels of headings in HTML. H1 is the highest level (it is normally the one with the largest font size and is used in the most important part of the page like titles), down to h6.

I am a h1 tag. I am the most important tag.

I am a h2 tag. I think I'm important too, if you ignore h1 who always steals my thunder.

I am a h3 tag. I have to live with these lot. Honestly.

I am a h4 tag. Perfectly middle-class(ish). But still a heading.

I am a h5 tag. Lucky to be here! ;)
(looks around) Oh, are you talking to me? I am the h6 tag. Mostly ignored, so I just do my own thing. Now off you go.
